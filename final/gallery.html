Take a Look at Different Study & Productivity Tools

Productivity Tools | Online Course Platforms

Note-taking App: Evernote stands out as a premier note-taking app not just for its basic functionality, but also for its array of extra features that are both extremely accessible and user-friendly. Whether you're a student, professional, these additional tools are designed to seemlessly enhance your productivity and organization. Evernote enhances note-taking with features including but not limited to, (1) the ability to insert audio recordings into notes, enriching content with voice memos or lectures. (2) It also allows for the insertion of calendar events, connecting directly to your calendar for streamlined organization and efficiency. (3) For those working with code, Evernote supports the insertion of code blocks, keeping snippets neatly formatted and easily accessible. (4) The navigation bar on the left side offers quick access to various sections such as shortcuts, notes, tasks, calendar events, notebooks, tags, shared content, and trash, ensuring a smooth and efficient user experience by keeping essential tools just a click away.

Structured Online Courses: Coursera is a global online learning platform that offers anyone, anywhere access to online courses and degrees from leading universities and companies. With its very user friendly interface, as well as its huge catalog of courses, Coursera provides learners with the tools they need to explore new knowledge and advance their careers. The picture above showcases what a course looks like on Coursera, along with the some of the important features it has readily available to users. (1) It displays details regarding the accessibility of the class, including its rating, giving learners insights into the course quality and learner satisfaction. (2) It highlights the skills participants will obtain from the course, mapping out the tangible benefits and learning outcomes. (3) Coursera supports courses having multiple language versions, which is invaluable if English is not your first language, making education more inclusive and accessible to a global audience. Coursera's myriad features make it a highly useful platform for personal and professional development, and these highlighted aspects are just a glimpse of what it offers to enhance the learning experience.

Online Video Learning: Khan Academy, a pioneer in providing free, high-quality education for anyone, anywhere, showcases its commitment through a platform rich with features designed to cater to diverse learning needs. The interface (highlighted in the leftmost image) allows efficient sorting of content by type and domain, ensuring learners quickly find resources like calculus, as shown with search results categorized for clarity. The sidebar (middle image), emphasized with a red circle, integrates video lessons and practice exercises in calculus, optimizing the learning flow and ensuring knowledge application. The questions section beneath each lesson (rightmost image) offers a collaborative space for inquiries, with sorting for top voted and most recent questions, enriching the interactive learning experience. Khan Academy's unique blend of comprehensive, free educational resources, including these thoughtful features, stands out as a testament to its unparalleled commitment to accessible education globally.

Project Management: Trello stands out as a highly effective project management tool, perfect for teams large and small. It packs a suite of features (1) to keep projects on track, such as organizing tasks, scheduling meetings, onboarding new members, brainstorming, and more, all shown in the visuals. The platform offers various membership options (2), from a cost-free plan to Standard, Premium, and Enterprise levels, making it easy for users to find a package that fits their needs without overspending—ideal for organizations with up to 5000 users. Additionally, Trello introduces advanced functionalities (3) like customizable project views, task automation, extra add-ons, templates, and app integrations, enhancing its utility and flexibility. This balance of features, combined with clear pricing, makes Trello a top choice for streamlining teamwork and project management in a user-friendly and adaptable manner.

Collaboration Tools: Microsoft Teams is a leading communication platform that brings together all the essential tools teams need to collaborate effectively. The Activity section keeps everyone updated with @mentions, reactions, and notifications, ensuring important messages don’t get missed. The Community section allows users to create spaces tailored to specific groups like businesses or sports teams, making it easy to foster a focused and engaging environment. Through the Chat section, Teams simplifies communication by integrating messaging and calls from one place, streamlining the way we connect without the need to schedule ahead. The Calendar section automatically updates with your meetings, whether you’ve scheduled them or been added by someone else, keeping your day organized effortlessly. Microsoft Teams is widely recognized for its comprehensive features and strong ecosystem, making it a top choice for companies looking to boost productivity and stay connected.





Online Course Platforms

Productivity Tools | Online Course Platforms

This section focuses on tools that can be classified as online course platforms. Dive into the important features which define and distinguish each of these tools, so you can find which one is right for you!


Coursera: Coursera is a top-notch online learning platform that works with universities and organizations worldwide to bring a wide variety of courses, professional certificates, and degrees in different fields. What makes Coursera special is its collaboration with well-known universities, offering courses created by their professors (1). This ensures the courses are thorough and come with certificates that can boost your career. Coursera also offers full bachelor’s and master’s degree programs (2) online, making it a go-to for higher education on the web. Plus, it’s committed to making learning available to everyone by providing financial aid and scholarships (3) to those who need them. In short, Coursera shines with its strong university partnerships, online degree programs, and support for students financially, setting it apart as a prime choice for both academic and professional growth.

edX: Edx, created by Harvard and MIT, is a nonprofit online platform offering college-level courses in various subjects. Its standout feature is the MicroMasters programs—graduate-level (1) courses aimed at boosting careers or leading into a full master's degree. Edx is also known for its Open edX, an open-source learning platform that lets other institutions offer courses (2), making education more accessible. Additionally, it provides XSeries Programs (3), put together by top experts and universities, which dive deep into particular topics. In essence, Edx is recognized for its MicroMasters and XSeries Programs, its commitment as a nonprofit, and its open-source approach, delivering quality education from well-known institutions.

Udemy: Udemy is a huge online platform for learning and teaching, offering courses in everything from business to personal growth. What makes it different is its marketplace approach (1), allowing people and experts to create and sell their own courses. Plus, when you buy a course on Udemy, you get to keep it forever, offering continuous learning opportunities as many creators on the platform update their material (2) as fields progress to keep their lessons relavant. There's also Udemy for Business (3), which provides customized learning paths for companies looking to boost their teams' skills. In short, Udemy stands out with its unique marketplace for courses, lifetime access for learners, and specialized solutions for corporate training, making it a go-to for both individual and business education needs.

Skillshare: Skillshare zeroes in on creative learning, with countless courses in areas like arts, design, and entrepreneurship. It’s all about the community here, with courses focusing on projects and forums (1) for feedback, making learning interactive. With a subscription, you get unlimited access to everything (2), pushing you to keep learning and trying new things. It's perfect for anyone creative (3), whether you're looking to improve a skill or starting fresh. To put it simply, Skillshare stands out for its hands-on, community-driven courses and its all-you-can-learn subscription, making it a top pick for creative minds wanting to grow.

LinkedIn Learning: LinkedIn Learning, which evolved from Lynda.com, offers video courses taught by experts in a range of professional areas. Its connection with LinkedIn means you get courses tailored to your professional profile (1) and can add completed courses to your profile, using your learning to boost your career. It has Learning Paths (2) to guide you through gaining specific skills or mastering software, all aimed at professional advancement. To top it off, it's a great choice for companies looking to support their employees' learning and growth (3). In short, LinkedIn Learning stands out with its LinkedIn integration, focused Learning Paths, and tools for company-wide learning, marking it as a prime platform for professional development.

MasterClass: MasterClass brings online classes led by famous faces and top experts in areas like the arts and business, blending skill-building with inspiration. Its unique draw is the courses taught by celebrities, giving you a peek into the successes of renowned individuals. Known for its excellent production quality, MasterClass makes learning not just informative but also really engaging. It offers an annual subscription, inviting you to dive into different subjects. In a nutshell, MasterClass sets itself apart with its star-studded teachers, high-quality videos, and a wide variety of topics, appealing to those looking for inspiration and premium learning experiences.
